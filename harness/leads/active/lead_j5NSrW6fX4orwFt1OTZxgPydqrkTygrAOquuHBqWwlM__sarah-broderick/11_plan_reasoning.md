# Sarah Broderick — Plan Reasoning

This document explains why the seven-day plan is structured the way it is, based only on the available lead folder data.

## Evidence Used

- `01_raw_lead.json`: Sarah Broderick, status `Maybe`, event date `2026-05-15`, guest count `25`, event type `Party`, owner tag `Andre`, opportunity flag `NEEDS VENUE NAME`, no phone number on contact.
- `02_continuity.jsonl`: lead created from live chat on `2026-05-05`, multiple outgoing emails, no captured inbound replies.
- `comms/email_2026-05-05_oz6czf76.json`: Andre sent menus/packages and service-style information; Sarah opened this email.
- `comms/email_2026-05-06_0lc0d8vo.json`: Andre sent a ballpark quote for Full Churrasco and Deluxe Churrasco; Sarah opened this email.
- `comms/email_2026-05-06_uckuhrom.json`: Andre followed up saying she can call him at any time.
- `03_comms_interpreted.md`, `04_profile.md`, `06_discovery.md`, `07_andre_alerts.md`, `08_client_ledger.md`: interpreted state repeatedly identifies the same constraints: no phone, no venue/address, no reply, event is close, and broad information has already been sent.

## Core Read

Sarah is not a cold lead. She gave a specific food request and opened the outreach. But she is also not yet a qualified sales conversation because the record lacks the practical details Andre needs: venue/address, serve time, service style, and phone number.

The existing outreach already did the education layer:

- menu/packages
- service styles
- tasting explanation
- ballpark pricing
- "you can call me anytime"

Because those emails already exist, the next plan should not send more PDFs, more menu material, or another broad "let us know" message. That would make the app look busy while not advancing the deal.

The plan should convert interest into a small commitment.

## Why The Plan Is Email-First

There is no phone number in Close. That means SMS and call actions are not truly executable yet. A plan that says "send SMS" before the number exists is fake action. The correct first objective is to get Sarah to provide a phone number.

Email is the only confirmed channel, and Sarah has opened email. So the plan uses email until the phone number appears.

## Why The First Ask Is Venue / Serve Time / Phone

The CRM explicitly flags `NEEDS VENUE NAME`. The date is close: May 15, with the sweep happening May 6. For catering, venue/address and food-ready time determine whether the quote is operationally real. Without those, Andre can keep talking but cannot confidently lock the job.

The three-field ask is:

1. venue/address or city
2. food-ready time
3. phone number

That is intentionally small. It avoids asking Sarah to solve every missing discovery slot at once.

## Why The Plan Does Not Lead With Tasting

The event is close, and Sarah asked for a party next Friday. A tasting may be useful later, but for this lead it is not the main conversion path. The main path is getting Andre on a quick call to confirm whether the quote/package direction works.

Offering tasting too early risks sending the conversation sideways when the urgency is actually logistics and commitment.

## Why The Plan Uses NEPQ Carefully

The strongest NEPQ question here is not "are you interested?" It is:

> When you said mainly Brazilian appetizers with some meat and rice, were you hoping for more of a lighter appetizer-heavy party menu, or closer to a full meal for everyone?

That question does three jobs:

- It proves Andre is listening to Sarah's exact words.
- It surfaces whether the churrasco quote matches the buyer's mental picture.
- It creates a natural reason for a call.

The plan uses this question on Day 3 after the direct logistics ask. That sequence matters: first try to get the operational blockers; if she still does not reply, ask the fit question that may reveal hesitation.

## Why The Plan Avoids Over-Pressure

Andre already sent a payment/lock-it-in style quote. The plan does not double down on pressure immediately because Sarah has not replied. Pressing harder could make the lead feel like she is being closed before she has been understood.

Instead, the plan frames the call as a shortcut:

- "avoid sending more generic info"
- "tell you plainly what is doable"
- "make the decision quickly"

That preserves urgency without turning the tone into a shove.

## The Deal Theory

Sarah likely has one of three states:

1. She is interested but has not had time to reply.
2. The quote direction is not quite aligned with what she imagined.
3. She is comparing options and needs a simpler path to decide.

The plan is built to work across all three:

- If she is interested, Day 1 makes reply easy.
- If the quote is misaligned, Day 3 opens the door to a lighter appetizer-heavy plan.
- If she is comparing, Days 4-6 make Comeketo feel like the provider who can simplify the decision fast.

## Success Definition

The plan succeeds when Sarah provides a phone number and agrees to a specific call window with Andre. It does not need to close the catering sale. This app's job is to get the lead to a real conversation with Andre, with enough context that the call can be useful.

Everything after that is Andre's job.
